27. Minimally invasive surgery refers to any surgical procedure that is performed through tiny incisions instead of a large opening. Because your surgeon will make smaller incisions, you will likely have a quicker recovery time and less pain than traditional open surgery but with the same benefits as traditional surgery. But not every surgery can be done with this method. Even where the minimally invasive surgery is an option , other factors such as your co-morbidities, weight, etc. can affect the choice. The doctor will weigh the benefits against the risks to make a judgement.


28.Early pregnancy scan- It is recommended between 7th to 12th week. It confirms singleton or multiple pregnancies (how many embryos are present), the viability of a pregnancy, the presence of a heartbeat, the gestation of pregnancy (number of weeks) by identifying the size of the pregnancy sac or the embryo, If the pregnancy is healthy by ruling out ectopic pregnancy and checking if the pregnancy sac is located within the uterus or not and the presence of any internal bleeding . NT scan -A nuchal translucency scan is done around the 12th week.The scan aims to measure the size of the nuchal fold at the back of the baby’s neck to rule out the possibility or risk of any chromosomal abnormalities. in combination with blood tests to determine the baby’s risk of having certain chromosomal abnormalities. Anomaly scan -It is done between 18 -21st weeks. Several measurements are taken to check the baby’s growth and development. The fetus is also assessed for any major anatomical abnormalities (such as problems with the head, limbs, heart and other internal organs). Growth scan-A growth scan is an ultrasound scan done during the last trimester(12 -28 weeks) of pregnancy to determine the growth of the baby. It can be done once or many times (as per the requirement). It checks if the baby is healthy by monitoring the baby’s moves, breath, and stretches, learns the position and weight of the baby, determines the amount of amniotic fluid present, check the position and maturity of the placenta, checks the position of umbilical cord, checks the wellbeing and circulation of the baby, learn how a previous caesarean scar looks, monitors twin pregnancy.
